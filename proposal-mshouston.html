A Note

From the team at White Haus Media.

To Adrienne Alexander, IPY Agency
And Briana Ambr'e Houston, M.Ed.

Briana has built something most creators never get close to. A 621,000-subscriber audience that listens, an actual micro-school that proves the curriculum works, and a body of original songs and printables that thousands of families now treat as part of their child's morning routine. That is not a content channel. That is an institution in early formation.

What we noticed is that the institution is now ahead of the website. Parents who find Briana on YouTube land on a homepage that loops the same paragraph three times. Printables sit behind a third-party iframe. Video requests run through a Google Form. The party supplies live on someone else's domain. Each of those is a small thing. Together they form a quiet tax on every visitor and every dollar that should be hers.

So we spent the last week building a concept, on our own time, with no obligation attached. We wanted to show what the next version of Learning with Ms. Houston could look like when the website carries the same weight as the channel.

The pages that follow walk through what we noticed, what is inside the concept, and how we would think about working together if there is interest. If there is not, the concept stays yours, and we wish her well. Either way, the work was a privilege.

Observations

Where the business meets the web.

Two observations from time spent inside Briana's brand. Each one shaped something in the pages that follow.

01

A rich product ecosystem, ready for a branded home.

Books on Amazon, printables on TPT, music on Spotify, party supplies through a trusted partner. Each of those distribution choices puts Briana where her audience already is, which is the right call. What is missing is a single branded surface that ties them together, so a parent finds the whole world of Ms. Houston in one place before they head to the platform that ships it.

02

DELA Academy is the most defensible asset, and it is invisible online.

Differentiated learning for K-5 students with autism, ADHD, and learning disabilities is a category with almost no quality digital product and parents who pay seriously for what works. The school exists. The web presence does not yet reflect it.

The Concept

Five additions to the foundation Briana already built.

Rather than describe the concept in slides, we built a working version on a live domain. The cards below outline what is inside it. The link to view it is at the end of this proposal, designed to be the last thing you see and the next step you take.

Recurring Revenue

The Scholars Club

A three-tier membership built so every family who wants Ms. Houston in their home can have her. Free starter pack, Scholar at nine dollars, Master Scholar at nineteen. Mission first. Recurring revenue as the byproduct.

Converts existing audience into ad-free, ongoing relationship.
B2B & Special Needs

The DELA Hub

A dedicated section for differentiated learning resources, organized by audience. Families of neurodivergent learners, special-needs co-ops and micro-schools, classroom teachers, and parents new to differentiation each find their own path.

Opens school district licensing and co-op revenue.
Productized Service

Branded Video Request Flow

A six-step modal replaces the Google Form. Pricing is transparent at $350, the rush fee is explained up front, and the experience matches the rest of the brand. Every field from the original form is captured, reformatted, and emailed to the team.

Removes friction from the highest-margin offer.
Unified Catalog

One Branded Storefront

Books, printables, party supplies, and music presented together in one place that feels unmistakably like Ms. Houston. Customers explore the full catalog inside the brand, then purchase wherever the product is sold, on Amazon, TPT, Spotify, or her trusted partner stores.

Distribution stays where it works. The discovery now happens at home.
Owned Infrastructure

Audience & IP Protection

Email capture replaces dependence on the YouTube algorithm. A footer signature establishes ownership of every asset on the site. The video request flow no longer routes through Google. The infrastructure now serves the mission rather than the other way around.

Reduces platform risk, builds audience Briana owns.
SEO Foundation

Search-Ready Architecture

Schema markup, semantic structure, and intent-mapped landing pages built in from day one. Three SEO plays prioritized: spillover search from YouTube viewers, free printables that compete with TPT and Pinterest, and educator-intent keywords that feed the DELA hub.

Compounding traffic that does not depend on the channel.

Every recommendation has to serve the audience first. Revenue follows trust, not the other way around.

The Engagement

How we shape the work.

There is no template engagement at White Haus Media. Briana's goals, runway, and where she wants the studio's involvement to begin and end are what shape the structure. What does not change are the principles inside every contract.

Project

The Build

A defined scope, three milestones, and a clean handoff at launch. The right shape when Briana wants the new infrastructure in place and prefers to take it from there.

Best for: clear deliverables, fixed timeline.
Most Common

Build & Partnership

The build paired with an ongoing monthly engagement covering content additions, search engine optimization, performance reviews, and infrastructure improvements. Sized to fit the goal.

Best for: ongoing momentum without rebuilding the relationship every few months.
Strategic

Performance Partnership

A reduced upfront investment paired with a performance share on net new recurring revenue. Designed for longer engagements where both sides want a deeper alignment of incentives.

Best for: shared upside, multi-year horizon.
How we contract. Every engagement is documented in plain language with intellectual property assigned to Briana, source code transferred at launch, an original work warranty, and a clear off-ramp at any point. Investment is shaped to scope and discussed in conversation, not assumed in a document.
